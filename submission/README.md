# Submission kit

Contents of this folder:

| File | Purpose | Audience |
|---|---|---|
| `cover_letter_AER.md` | Two-page cover letter targeted at *American Economic Review* | Editor, managing editor |
| `response_to_referee_kit.md` | Pre-emptive responses to seven likely referee objections | Internal preparation — **do not submit** |
| `policy_brief.md` | One-page plain-language summary | CEA / Treasury / IMF / press |

## Workflow

1. **Before submission**: read `response_to_referee_kit.md` and address any items flagged `[to be added]` in the paper (robustness appendices, CES utility check, placebo tests).
2. **Submission package**: adapt `cover_letter_AER.md` (change the editor name, date, and any seminar history), convert to PDF, and include alongside `paper/main.pdf`.
3. **Press / policy outreach**: send `policy_brief.md` to relevant policy offices and journalists after the paper is publicly posted (SSRN, NBER, author website).
4. **Other journals**: if pivoting to *QJE* / *ReStud* / *JPE* / *Econometrica*, copy `cover_letter_AER.md` to `cover_letter_<journal>.md` and re-tune the "Why [journal]" paragraph.

## Journal fit notes

- **AER (default)**: General interest, policy-relevant, prizes empirical-theoretical integration. Best fit.
- **QJE**: Prizes strong empirical identification and fresh theoretical reframing. Needs stronger emphasis on the quasi-experimental design in Section 5.
- **ReStud**: Bilbiie (2025) lives here — natural home for the analytical extensions, but make sure the empirical and quantitative story is equally prominent.
- **JPE**: Prizes quantitative-model innovation; good fit if we emphasize the SSJ-HANK solver and its extensions.
- **Econometrica**: Prizes methodological depth; needs more formal identification theory if aiming here.
